ABOUT ASCB

Andres Soriano Colleges of Bislig (ASCB)

Type: Private

Religious affiliation: Non-Sectarian

Term structure: Semester, Trimester

Andres Soriano Colleges of Bislig, a non-stock, non-profit, non-sectarian educational institution, aspires to form a community of totally liberated persons equipped with knowledge, skills and values in a just and humane society.

It aims to deliver quality, foster awareness, personal desipline and ability to make creative and sound judgement smong it's students, and to stand as a training ground for well-balanced academic education designed to promote a culture of excellence.

Academics

ASCB provides a diverse array of educational opportunities, including 12 Bachelor's programs, a Master's degree program, and a Certificate Course. Additionally, the institute offers comprehensive basic education aligned with the K-12 program. See Departments to learn more these programs.

History

The Andres Soriano College of Bislig was founded in 1951 as a consequence of the tenacious efforts of people who thought that children should be developed holistically. The college, which was situated in Mangagoy, was established to meet the educational requirements of the expanding populace, which included workers from businesses like PICOP and BBLCI. Under the direction of committed civic-minded people like Mr. Godofredo M. Manila and Mr. Marcelo Ligas, Andres Soriano College started out as a modest 13-room nipa building that could house hundreds of eager students.
