ABOUT ASCB

Andres Soriano Colleges of Bislig, Inc. (ASCB) is a private, non-sectarian school in the province of Surigao del Sur.

HISTORY

In response to the demand for a local high school in Mangangoy, Bislig, the South East Pacific Institute now Andres Soriano Colleges of Bislig was built in 1952.

A fully developed community of integrated persons as agents in the transformation of society in Bislig City and throughout the land.

Andres Soriano College, a non-stock, non-profit, non-sectarian educational institution, aspires to form a community of totally liberated persons equipped with knowledge, skills and values in a just and humane society. It aims to embrace the primary existence of education, fosters awareness, personal discipline and ability to make creative and sound judgment and stands as the training ground for a well-balanced academic education designed to promote a culture of excellence.

The Andres Soriano College of Bislig was founded in 1951 as a consequence of the tenacious efforts of people who thought that children should be developed holistically. The college, which was situated in Mangagoy, was established to meet the educational requirements of the expanding populace, which included workers from businesses like PICOP and BBLCI. Under the direction of committed civic-minded people like Mr. Godofredo M. Manila and Mr. Marcelo Ligas, Andres Soriano College started out as a modest 13-room nipa building that could house hundreds of eager students.
